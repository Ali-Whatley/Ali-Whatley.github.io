Welcome! I'm a graduate student at the Sam Nunn School of International Affairs at Georgia Tech, where I'm pursuing an MS in International Affairs with tracks in Science & Technology and International Security.

My research focuses on defense policy, nuclear governance, and energy security. I'm currently working on a project examining organizational decision-making in high-risk technological systems, using the 1954 Castle Bravo nuclear test as a case study.

Before Tech, I completed dual degrees in Economics and Political Science at Georgia State University, along with MA-level coursework in Economics through the 4+1 program.

Originally from Halifax, Nova Scotia, I've spent most of my life in Atlanta, Georgia. When I'm not researching, you'll usually find me reading (mostly nonfiction) or watching old movies.
