I'm a graduate student at Georgia Tech pursuing an MS in International Affairs with tracks in Science & Technology and International Security. I spend a lot of time thinking about energy security, economic statecraft, technological proliferation, and the political economy of defense.

My current work includes an ASDL Grand Challenge project on energy systems and national security, where I'm developing tabletop tools to help policymakers make quantitative decisions for tactical responses to energy shocks. I also collaborate with the Pollitik lab on political approval ratings and electoral dynamics.

Before Georgia Tech, I completed a dual degree in Economics and Political Science at Georgia State University. I interned with the Bureau of Labor Statistics working on time series analysis and R code development.

I also write and tweet occasionally. You can follow my full media diet here.
