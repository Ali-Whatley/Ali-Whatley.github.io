Research

My research focuses on defense policy, technological proliferation, energy security, and the political economy of defense. I'm particularly interested in how organizational structures shape decision-making around high-risk technologies, and how states build and sustain the industrial capacity for conflict.

Working Papers

"Nuclear Testing and Normal Accidents: A Case Study of the Castle Bravo Accident"
Independent study with Dr. Margaret E. Kosal, Sam Nunn School of International Affairs, Georgia Tech

Examines the interaction between organizational structure and technological complexity in nuclear governance, using the 1954 Castle Bravo accident as a case study.
