Research

Current Projects

ASDL Grand Challenge: Energy Systems and National Security

Year-long group research project focused on creating tabletop tools to aid policymakers in making quantitative decisions for tactical responses to energy shocks. The project bridges systems engineering principles with international security analysis.

Pollitik - Political Popularity Project

Collaborative work with the Pollitik lab utilizing the Executive Approval Project database to facilitate comparative estimation of nations' electorates. Recent posts include analysis of presidential approval dynamics in various contexts.

Past Research

An Examination of Natural Gas Deregulation

Semester-long economics research project examining natural gas pricing dynamics following deregulation of natural gas markets. Analysis used econometric methods to assess market efficiency and price formation mechanisms.

Research Interests

My work sits at the intersection of economics and international security, with particular focus on:

Energy Security: Strategic implications of energy infrastructure, supply chain vulnerabilities, and geopolitical dimensions of energy markets.

Economic Statecraft: How states use economic tools as instruments of foreign policy, including sanctions, trade policy, and technology controls.

Technological Proliferation: Diffusion of emerging technologies with security implications, export controls, and technology transfer dynamics.

Political Economy of Defense: Defense industrial base dynamics, procurement decisions, and economic dimensions of military capability.
